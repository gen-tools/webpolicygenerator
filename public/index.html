Free Privacy Policy Generator for Small Businesses

Running a small website or shop online is already enough work. You’ve got products to manage, emails to answer, content to keep fresh, and a dozen other tasks waiting for you every morning. The last thing most people want to think about? Legal pages.

But here’s the truth: if you’re collecting emails, letting people check out with credit cards, or even just running Google Analytics, you’re dealing with personal data. And when you deal with data, the law expects you to explain what you’re doing with it. That’s where a privacy policy comes in.

Skip it, and you’re not only risking fines under rules like GDPR or CCPA — you’re also telling visitors you don’t take their information seriously. That alone can be enough to make them click away.

That’s exactly why I built WebPolicyGenerator. It’s a privacy policy generator made for people like you and me, people who don’t speak legal jargon, don’t want to pay a lawyer hundreds of dollars, and just need something that works.

Why Privacy Policies Matter

Let’s break it down in simple words.

GDPR (for Europe) and CCPA (for California) are strict. If you’ve got visitors from these places, you’re expected to explain exactly how you handle their data.

Canada has PIPEDA. Australia has its own Privacy Act. More countries are adding rules every year.

Even if you’re just one person with a blog, these laws can still apply to you.

But there’s another reason: trust.

Imagine walking into a coffee shop that asks for your phone number and email just to order a drink, without explaining why. You’d feel uneasy. Online, people feel the same. When they see a clear privacy page, it shows you’re transparent. When they don’t, doubts creep in.
